DDT

Chinese: 滴滴涕;  French: DDT (n.m.);  Russian: ДДТ

Status: ISO 1750 (published)
IUPAC PIN: 1,1′-(2,2,2-trichloroethane-1,1-diyl)bis(4-chlorobenzene)
IUPAC name: 1,1,1-trichloro-2,2-bis(4-chlorophenyl)ethane
CAS name: 1,1′-(2,2,2-trichloroethylidene)bis[4-chlorobenzene]
CAS Reg. No.: 50-29-3
Formula: C14H9Cl5
Activity: acaricides (organochlorine)
insecticides (organochlorine)
Notes: DDT is the common name for the technical product; the pure compound has the ISO common name pp′-DDT.
The acronym is derived from an old and imprecise name, 4,4′-dichlorodiphenyltrichloroethane.
The name “clofenotane” is approved by the World Health Organization; the Chinese version is 滴滴涕 and the Russian version is клофенотан.
The name “dicophane” was formerly approved by the British Pharmacopoeia Commission.
